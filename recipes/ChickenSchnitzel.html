Chicken Schnitzel

Description

Chicken Schnitzel is a thin, breaded, and fried chicken breast dish. It's made by pounding the chicken, coating it in flour, egg, and breadcrumbs, then frying until crispy. Served with lemon wedges, it's light and flavorful. Originating in Central Europe, it's a classic and popular dish.

Nutrional Information

Calories : 268 Fat : 8g Carbs : 25g Protein : 23g

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ½ tablespoons honey
  • 1 ½ tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 ¼ tablespoons soy sauce
  • ½ tablespoon crushed garlic
  • 1 ½ cups bread crumbs (or as needed)
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 6 boneless chicken breast halves, pounded very thin
  • ½ lemon, cut into wedges

Steps

  1. Beat together eggs, honey, Dijon mustard, soy sauce, and minced garlic in a mixing bowl.
  2. Combine bread crumbs, salt, and pepper in a shallow dish for coating.
  3. Heat 1/2 to 1 inch of vegetable oil in a heavy skillet over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C), or use a deep fryer.
  4. Dip each chicken breast into the egg mixture, pressing to ensure full coverage, then coat evenly in the bread crumb mixture.
  5. Fry the chicken in batches, using tongs to carefully place and flip, until golden brown and fully cooked through, about 3 to 4 minutes per side.
  6. Drain excess oil on paper towels and serve with fresh lemon wedges on the side.
