Chilli Paneer

Description

Chilli panner is a spicy Indian street food made from grilled paneer tossed in a tangy chili sauce. Originating in the Indian subcontinent, it features a crispy texture and bold flavors. Loved by spice enthusiasts, it blends creamy paneer with fiery heat.

Nutrional Information

Calories : 302 Fat : 9g Carbs : 35g Protein : 23g

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cubed paneer (Indian cheese curd)
  • ⅛ cup cornstarch, or as needed
  • ⅛ cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ (1 inch) piece fresh ginger, grated
  • ½ onion, chopped
  • 2 green chile peppers, halved
  • ½ bell pepper, diced

    • Gravy:

  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chile sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato sauce
  • ½ teaspoon white sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon water, or as needed (Optional)
  • ⅛ cup chopped fresh cilantro, or to taste
  • 1 green onions, chopped (Optional)

Steps

  1. Toss paneer in a bowl with enough cornstarch to coat, mixing well.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat, then fry the paneer until golden brown on each side, about 1 to 2 minutes per side. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate and reserve the oil in the skillet.
  3. Add minced garlic and grated ginger to the hot oil, then stir in chopped onion, halved green chile peppers, and diced bell pepper. Cook and stir until the vegetables are golden brown, about 5 minutes.
  4. Combine soy sauce, chile sauce, tomato sauce, sugar, salt, and remaining cornstarch in the skillet. Stir in water if the mixture is too thick. Add the paneer and cook, stirring until the gravy is thickened, about 2 to 3 minutes.
  5. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh cilantro and chopped green onions.
